PINK PAPPUSGRASS
Pappophorum bicolor Fourn.
Plant Symbol = PABI2

Description
Grass Family (Poaceae). Pink pappusgrass is a
native, warm-season, perennial bunch grass. The
height ranges from 1 to 2 feet. The leaf blade is
narrow; flat or slightly rolled inward, giving a
threadlike appearance when dry. The leaf sheath is
mostly basal, rounded, and shorter than the
internodes. The ligule has a tuft of long hairs. The
seedhead is a spike-like, pinkish panicle 4 to 5 inches
long. The lemmas are somewhat hard and the end is
dissected into about 12 awns of unequal length about
as long as lemmas with the glumes transparent.

Management
This grass is seldom used as a key management
species. It responds to proper use and deferred
grazing during the growing season.

Establishment
Growth starts in late spring and early summer.
Seedheads appear about 6 weeks later. Leaves
remain green until the early fall. It reproduces from
other seeds. It seldom grows in pure stands and
grows best on deep loam to clay loam soils that are
subject to overflow.
